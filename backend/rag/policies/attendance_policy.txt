ATTENDANCE POLICY — DEPARTMENT OF ENGINEERING
Rajiv Gandhi Institute of Technology
Academic Year 2025-2026

1. MINIMUM ATTENDANCE REQUIREMENT
Students are required to maintain a minimum of 75% attendance in each subject individually. Attendance is calculated separately for theory classes and practical/laboratory sessions. A student falling below 75% in any subject will be considered as having an attendance deficiency.

2. SEVERE DEFICIENCY THRESHOLD
Students with attendance below 60% in any subject are classified as having a severe attendance deficiency. Such students are automatically barred from appearing in the end-semester examination for that subject unless they successfully appeal to the Academic Committee with valid documented reasons.

3. CONDONATION OF ATTENDANCE
Condonation up to 10% (i.e., effective minimum attendance of 65%) may be granted in extraordinary circumstances such as:
  - Hospitalization with medical certificate issued by a registered medical practitioner
  - Death of an immediate family member (parent, sibling, spouse)
  - Participation in inter-university or national-level sports/cultural events with prior approval
  - Natural calamity or government-declared emergency

Condonation applications must be submitted to the Head of Department within 7 days of the student's return. No condonation will be granted retrospectively after the end of the semester.

4. ATTENDANCE MONITORING AND REPORTING
Attendance is recorded by the subject teacher at the beginning of every lecture and practical session. Monthly attendance reports are generated by the Academic Office and displayed on the institution's notice board as well as the student portal.

Students whose cumulative attendance falls below 80% in any subject will receive an automated warning notification via the institution's Early Warning System (EWS). Parents or guardians will be notified by SMS and email upon the first warning.

5. SHORTAGE OF ATTENDANCE — CONSEQUENCES
- Below 75% in any one subject: Student is placed on the EWS Attendance Watch List. Mentor counselling is mandatory.
- Below 65% in two or more subjects: Student is issued a formal written warning and placed on Academic Probation.
- Below 60% in any subject: Student is detained from appearing in end-semester exams for that subject. Re-registration for the subject in the next semester is required, affecting graduation timelines.

6. MEDICAL LEAVE AND EXTENDED ABSENCE
Students on extended medical leave (more than 10 consecutive working days) must inform their class mentor and HOD in writing. Medical documents must be submitted to the Academic Office. Attendance for such periods may be excluded from the calculation at the discretion of the Academic Committee.

7. LEAVE OF ABSENCE FOR OFFICIAL ACTIVITIES
Students representing the institution in inter-collegiate competitions, NSS/NCC activities, or industrial visits approved by the department will not be penalized for attendance. Such absences must be pre-approved by the Head of Department.

8. RESPONSIBILITY OF STUDENTS
It is the sole responsibility of the student to track their attendance regularly using the student portal. Ignorance of attendance shortage will not be accepted as a reason for non-compliance. Students are advised to check their attendance status at least once per week.

9. APPEALS AND GRIEVANCE REDRESSAL
Students who believe their attendance has been incorrectly recorded may submit a grievance in writing to the class teacher within 5 working days of the discrepancy. The teacher must resolve the discrepancy within 3 working days. Unresolved disputes may be escalated to the Head of Department.

10. AMENDMENTS
This policy is reviewed and updated annually by the Academic Council. The institution reserves the right to make amendments to this policy at any time during the academic year, with prior notice to students.
