REMEDIAL TEACHING AND ACADEMIC SUPPORT GUIDELINES
Rajiv Gandhi Institute of Technology
Academic Support & Student Success Office

1. PURPOSE
These guidelines define the framework for identifying students who need additional academic support and delivering structured remedial interventions. The objective is to ensure that no academically capable student is left behind due to temporary difficulties or gaps in foundational knowledge.

2. ELIGIBILITY FOR REMEDIAL SUPPORT
A student is eligible for remedial teaching if they meet any one or more of the following criteria:
  a) Average assessment score below 60% in any subject across Unit Tests 1 and 2
  b) Marks below 40% in any single Unit Test
  c) Failure to submit two or more assignments consecutively
  d) Flagged as YELLOW or RED in the institutional Early Warning System (EWS)
  e) Self-referral or referral by class mentor or subject teacher

3. TYPES OF REMEDIAL SUPPORT

3.1 Subject-Specific Remedial Classes
Extra classes conducted by the subject teacher outside regular hours, targeting specific topics where students are weak. These are held typically on Saturdays between 9:00 AM and 12:00 PM. Attendance at remedial classes is recorded but is treated separately from the regular attendance register.

3.2 Peer Tutoring Program
High-performing students (first division with distinction) are invited to participate as peer tutors. Peer tutors receive academic credit and a certificate of participation. Peer tutoring sessions are conducted in the department library or designated tutoring rooms, up to 1 hour per session, 3 sessions per week per subject.

3.3 One-on-One Mentor Sessions
Each student is assigned a faculty mentor at the time of admission. Students flagged by EWS are mandated to attend at least one mentor counselling session per month. Mentors document the outcome of each session and submit a monthly report to the Head of Department.

3.4 Online Resource Guidance
Subject teachers must recommend specific video lectures (NPTEL, MIT OpenCourseWare), practice platforms, and reference materials to struggling students. These recommendations should be personalized based on the student's identified weak areas.

4. IDENTIFICATION PROCESS
The Academic Early Warning System (EWS) generates a risk assessment for each student at the end of every assessment cycle (Unit Test 1, Unit Test 2, and pre-finals). The system flags students in three categories:
  - GREEN: No intervention required
  - YELLOW: Mentor counselling and optional remedial classes recommended
  - YELLOW: Close monitoring; mandatory remedial attendance if score < 50%
  - RED: Mandatory remedial classes, mentor counselling, and guardian notification

5. PROCESS FOR REMEDIAL CLASS ENROLLMENT
  Step 1: EWS generates list of flagged students after each assessment.
  Step 2: Class mentor contacts each flagged student individually within 3 working days.
  Step 3: Student and mentor agree on a remedial plan (subjects, schedule, type of support).
  Step 4: Remedial plan is documented and submitted to the Academic Office.
  Step 5: Progress is reviewed after 4 weeks. If no improvement is observed, escalation to HOD.

6. TRACKING PROGRESS
Remedial attendance and subsequent assessment performance are tracked by the EWS. A comparison of pre-remedial and post-remedial assessment scores is generated by the system. If a remedial student's next assessment score does not improve by at least 10%, the intervention is escalated.

7. STUDENT RESPONSIBILITIES
Students enrolled in remedial programs must:
  - Attend all scheduled sessions unless there is a documented emergency
  - Complete all assignments and practice problems given during remedial sessions
  - Maintain communication with their mentor throughout the intervention period
  - Not treat remedial sessions as a substitute for regular classes

8. FACULTY RESPONSIBILITIES
Subject teachers and mentors conducting remedial sessions must:
  - Prepare targeted lesson plans based on each student's specific weak areas
  - Submit session notes and progress updates to the Academic Office within 2 days of each session
  - Escalate students showing no improvement or non-compliance to the HOD within 2 weeks

9. CONFIDENTIALITY
Participation in remedial programs is treated with full confidentiality. Session notes and individual student performance data are accessible only to the student's mentor, subject teacher, HOD, and Academic Office staff. This information is not shared with peers or in public forums.

10. REVIEW AND IMPROVEMENT
This policy is reviewed each semester by the Academic Council in consultation with the Student Success Office. Feedback from students and faculty participating in remedial programs is collected anonymously and used to improve the program each year.
