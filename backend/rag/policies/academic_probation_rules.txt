ACADEMIC PROBATION POLICY
Rajiv Gandhi Institute of Technology
Academic Regulations — As per University Statutes (Revised 2024)

1. DEFINITION OF ACADEMIC PROBATION
Academic Probation is a formal designation applied to a student whose academic performance or conduct has fallen below the institution's minimum acceptable standards. A student on Academic Probation is enrolled under specific conditions and must demonstrate measurable improvement within the probationary period to continue their studies at the institution.

2. CONDITIONS TRIGGERING ACADEMIC PROBATION

A student shall be placed on Academic Probation if any one or more of the following conditions are met:

2.1 Attendance-Based Triggers:
  a) Attendance below 65% in two or more subjects in the same semester
  b) Attendance below 60% in any single subject for two consecutive semesters
  c) Detention from end-semester examination in more than one subject in a semester

2.2 Academic Performance-Based Triggers:
  a) SGPA (Semester Grade Point Average) below 5.0 on a 10-point scale for two consecutive semesters
  b) Failure in more than 50% of registered subjects in any given semester
  c) Average assessment score below 40% across all subjects in a semester
  d) EWS risk category of RED in two consecutive assessment cycles within the same semester

2.3 Subject Attempt-Based Triggers:
  a) Having exhausted more than 80% of maximum permitted attempts for any subject
  b) Re-registration required for the same subject for the third time

2.4 Financial-Based Triggers:
  a) Fee dues outstanding for more than 60 days after the payment deadline
  b) Fee defaulter status without an approved installment plan

3. PROBATION PERIOD
Academic Probation is issued for one academic semester. At the end of the probationary semester, the student's performance is reviewed by the Academic Probation Committee. Probation may be:
  - Lifted: If the student meets all conditions set in the Probation Agreement
  - Extended: For one additional semester if partial improvement is demonstrated
  - Escalated to Dismissal Proceedings: If no meaningful improvement is observed

4. NOTICE AND COMMUNICATION
  Step 1: Student receives a formal written notice from the Academic Office within 10 working days of the triggering event.
  Step 2: Guardian is notified via formal letter (Tier 2/3 under Guardian Notification Policy).
  Step 3: A Probation Agreement meeting is scheduled within 5 working days of the notice. Attendees: student, guardian (if available), class mentor, and HOD.
  Step 4: A Probation Agreement document is signed by the student and mentor. This document outlines:
      - Specific performance targets by mid-semester and end-semester
      - Mandatory attendance at remedial classes
      - Minimum attendance target (must reach 80% within 4 weeks)
      - Mandatory weekly mentor check-in
      - Conditions for probation to be lifted

5. ACADEMIC RESTRICTIONS DURING PROBATION
Students on Academic Probation are subject to the following restrictions:
  a) May not register for more than 75% of the maximum permitted credits for the semester
  b) May not hold any elected student body position or serve as club/society president
  c) May not represent the institution in external competitions without explicit approval from HOD and Dean
  d) Are required to attend all mentor counselling sessions (no opt-out)
  e) Are subject to bi-weekly progress reviews by the Academic Probation Committee

6. SUPPORT PROVIDED DURING PROBATION
The institution is committed to supporting probationary students through:
  a) Access to all remedial teaching programs (see Remedial Teaching Guidelines)
  b) Priority appointment slots with the Student Counselling Cell
  c) Financial counselling if fee-related issues are a contributing factor
  d) Access to the Student Welfare Fund for eligible students facing financial hardship
  e) Career guidance and academic planning sessions to help the student develop a realistic path forward

7. LIFTING OF PROBATION
Academic Probation is formally lifted when the student:
  a) Achieves a semester SGPA of 6.0 or above at the end of the probationary semester
  b) Achieves 75% or above attendance in all registered subjects
  c) Clears all outstanding fee dues or has an approved repayment plan in place
  d) Completes all mandatory remedial sessions and mentor check-ins
  e) Receives a confirmation from the Academic Probation Committee

8. ESCALATION TO ACADEMIC DISMISSAL
If a student on Academic Probation fails to meet the required targets and does not demonstrate good-faith effort, the case is referred to the Academic Disciplinary Committee for consideration of:
  - Suspension for one semester
  - Withdrawal from the degree program

Dismissal decisions are approved by the Principal and communicated in writing. Students have the right to appeal to the University Grievance Redressal Committee within 15 working days of the dismissal notice.

9. APPEAL PROCESS
A student who believes Academic Probation has been incorrectly applied may submit a written appeal to the Dean of Academic Affairs within 10 working days of receiving the probation notice. The appeal must clearly state the grounds for contestation and include supporting evidence. The Dean's decision, communicated within 15 working days, is final at the institutional level.

10. RECORD KEEPING AND CONFIDENTIALITY
Academic Probation status is recorded in the student's academic history file. This information is shared only with the student, guardian, relevant faculty, and institutional administration. It is not disclosed to external parties (employers, other universities) without the student's prior written consent, except when legally required.

11. REVIEW OF POLICY
This policy is reviewed annually by the Academic Council and updated in accordance with the guidelines issued by the All India Council for Technical Education (AICTE), the affiliating university, and the National Education Policy (NEP 2020) framework.
